Description

1984 is a dystopian novel set in a totalitarian future where the world is divided among oppressive super‑states, and the super‑state Oceania, ruled by the omnipresent Big Brother and governed by the rigid ideology of Ingsoc , watches, controls and manipulates every aspect of individual life. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

The story follows Winston Smith, a low‑ranking employee of the Ministry of Truth, whose job is to rewrite historical records to fit the Party’s propaganda. As Winston secretly rebels, through forbidden love with Julia, private thoughts and a longing for truth, he becomes entangled in the brutal mechanisms of control: surveillance, censorship, thought‑police, torture and reality manipulation. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

1984 remains a chilling and powerful warning about the dangers of totalitarianism, surveillance, propaganda and the loss of personal freedom. Its themes, manipulation of truth, control of language and history, erosion of individuality, remain deeply relevant decades after its publication. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
